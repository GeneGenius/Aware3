Things To Do
Make the most of your time in Southern Africa
Eswatini

A jewel of Southern Africa, Eswatini packs extraordinary wildlife, rich Swazi culture and stunning landscapes into one of the continent's smallest kingdoms. Here are our favourite ways to explore it.

Hlane Royal National Park
One of Swaziland's largest game reserves — home to lions, elephants, rhinos, hippos and hundreds of bird species. Perfect for a safari day trip.
Nature
Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary
A peaceful wildlife sanctuary near Lobamba. Guests can hike, cycle or ride horses through the reserve alongside hippos and antelope.
Nature
Mantenga Cultural Village
An authentic living Swazi village showcasing traditional architecture, dance performances and local crafts. A must-visit for cultural immersion.
Culture
Sibebe Rock
The world's second largest exposed granite dome. A challenging and rewarding hike with breathtaking panoramic views over the kingdom.
Adventure
Swazi Candles Craft Market
Famous for hand-crafted candles in vibrant shapes and colours. A great place to pick up unique souvenirs and local artwork.
Shopping
Malolotja Nature Reserve
Stunning highland wilderness with waterfalls, ancient cycad forests and excellent hiking trails. One of Eswatini's most scenic natural areas.
Nature
Ezulwini Valley
The Valley of Heaven — a lush stretch of land housing the royal palace, craft markets, spas and the famous Gables Shopping Centre.
Culture
Phophonyane Falls
A beautiful private nature reserve with a stunning waterfall, forest trails and an eco-lodge. Perfect for a quiet day in nature.
Nature
Johannesburg

South Africa's vibrant city of gold is just a few hours away and well worth a few days. From world-class museums to buzzing neighbourhoods and wildlife just outside the city — Joburg rewards curious travellers.

Apartheid Museum
One of the most powerful museums in the world — a moving journey through South Africa's apartheid era and the road to democracy. Essential viewing.
Culture
Soweto
The iconic township that shaped South Africa's history. Visit Vilakazi Street, home to both Nelson Mandela and Desmond Tutu, and explore vibrant local restaurants and shebeens.
Culture
Constitution Hill
A living museum and working court built on the site of the old Johannesburg Fort prison. Includes the Old Fort, Women's Jail and the Constitutional Court.
Culture
Gold Reef City
A theme park built on a historic gold mine. Includes rides, a casino, period buildings and underground mine tours showing how gold was mined.
Adventure
Neighbourgoods Market
Braamfontein's famous Saturday market — the best local food, craft beer, coffee and artisan goods all in one vibrant spot. A true Joburg institution.
Food
Lion & Safari Park
Drive-through safari just 45 minutes from the city centre featuring lions, cheetahs, wild dogs, giraffes and more. Great for families.
Nature
Maboneng Precinct
Joburg's coolest creative neighbourhood — art galleries, independent restaurants, rooftop bars and weekend markets in a beautifully regenerated urban precinct.
Culture
Cradle of Humankind
A UNESCO World Heritage Site containing some of the world's oldest hominid fossils. The Maropeng visitor centre tells the story of human origins.
History
